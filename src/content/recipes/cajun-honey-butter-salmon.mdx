ingredients_subsections:
  - title: Cajun Seasoning
    items:
      - 2 tsp garlic powder
      - 2 tsp smoked paprika
      - 2 tsp dried Italian seasoning
      - 1 tsp cayenne pepper
      - 1 tsp onion powder
      - ½ tsp finely ground black pepper
      - ½ tsp salt
      - ½ tsp crushed red pepper flakes
instructions:
  - In a small bowl mix together cajun seasoning ingredients
  - Use electric hand mixer to whip butter for 2 minutes until light and fluffy. Add honey and cream together until smooth. Mix in 1 tablespoon of the cajun seasoning
  - Pat salmon fillets dry on both sides with a paper towel. Rub with oil, then season with salt and pepper to taste.
  - Bake salmon on at 400 degrees until 145 degrees temperature, ~15 minutes. You can grill instead of baking as well.
  - Place a generous dollop of cajun honey butter on top of each fillet.
  - Allow to melt for about 30 seconds, then use the back of a spoon to spread the butter all over the the top of the salmon
  - Garnish with chopped cilantro or parsley and serve immediately.
notes:
  - Salmon is done when it reaches an internal temperature of 145°F.
  - You can adjust the spice level by reducing the cayenne pepper and red pepper flakes.
  - Store leftover cajun seasoning in an airtight container for future use.

## The Perfect Balance of Sweet and Spicy

This Cajun Honey Butter Salmon brings together the robust flavors of Louisiana-inspired spices with the delicate sweetness of honey butter, creating a dish that's both sophisticated and comforting. The beauty of this recipe lies in its contrasting elements – the crispy salmon skin, tender flesh, and the rich, melting butter that forms a luscious sauce as it mingles with the spices.

The Cajun seasoning blend typically includes paprika, cayenne, garlic powder, onion powder, and herbs like thyme and oregano, providing a complex flavor profile that stands up beautifully to the richness of the salmon. When combined with sweet honey and creamy butter, it creates a harmonious balance that elevates this simple fish dish to restaurant quality with minimal effort.

## Mastering the Perfect Salmon Sear

The key to this dish is properly searing the salmon to achieve a crispy skin and moist interior. Here are some tips for perfecting your technique:

- **Choose the right pan**: A cast-iron skillet provides the best heat retention and creates an excellent sear
- **Pat salmon dry**: Removing excess moisture helps achieve a better crust
- **Start with skin-side down**: This protects the delicate flesh while the skin crisps up
- **Don't rush it**: Let the salmon cook undisturbed for the full time to develop a proper crust
- **Know when it's done**: Look for opaque flesh that flakes easily with a fork, but still has a slightly translucent center for medium doneness

The final touch of adding the Cajun honey butter to the hot salmon creates an instant sauce as it melts, coating the fish in a glossy, flavorful glaze that's both beautiful and delicious.

## Serving Suggestions

This Cajun honey butter salmon pairs beautifully with a variety of sides:

- **Greens**: Serve over wilted spinach or a simple arugula salad dressed with lemon
- **Grains**: Accompany with steamed rice, quinoa, or creamy grits for a Southern-inspired meal
- **Vegetables**: Roasted asparagus, broccolini, or green beans make excellent companions
- **Potatoes**: Creamy mashed potatoes or crispy roasted potatoes soak up the delicious butter sauce

For a complete Louisiana-inspired feast, serve with a side of dirty rice and collard greens.

## Variations

### Blackened Cajun Salmon
For an even more traditional Louisiana approach, coat the salmon more heavily with Cajun spices and cook at a higher heat to achieve a "blackened" exterior. Be sure to have good ventilation!

### Citrus Cajun Salmon
Add orange or lime zest to the honey butter for a citrus twist that brightens the rich flavors.

### Herbed Cajun Salmon
Incorporate fresh herbs like dill, chives, or cilantro into the honey butter for added freshness and color.
