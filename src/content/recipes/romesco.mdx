instructions:
  - Preheat the oven to 350°F.
  - On a rimmed baking sheet, spread out the almonds and hazelnuts in an even layer. Toast until lightly browned and aromatic, 10-12 minutes. Remove from oven and let cool.
  - Increase the oven temperature to 450°F.
  - Place the bell peppers and tomatoes on a rimmed baking sheet and roast, turning occasionally, until charred on all sides and starting to soften. Alternatively, you can use a grill or a grill pan. Transfer the vegetables to a bowl, cover with a kitchen towel, and allow them to steam. When cool enough to handle, slip off the skins with a towel and remove the seeds.
  - In a food processor, combine the cooled almonds and hazelnuts, roasted vegetables, parsley, garlic confit, and olive oil. Process to make a coarse sauce with some chunks remaining. Add the vinegar and season with salt and pepper.

## Origins of Romesco

Romesco sauce originates from Tarragona, in the Catalonia region of Spain. It was originally created by fishermen to accompany the local catch of the day. The bright, complex flavor comes from the combination of sweet roasted peppers, tangy tomatoes, and the richness of nuts and olive oil.

What makes romesco special is its versatility and depth of flavor - the smokiness from the paprika, tanginess from the vinegar, and richness from the nuts and olive oil create a perfectly balanced sauce that enhances nearly anything it touches.

## Serving Suggestions

Romesco is incredibly versatile and can be used in many ways:

- **Seafood Accompaniment**: Traditionally served with grilled or roasted fish
- **Vegetable Dip**: Perfect with grilled or raw vegetables
- **Sandwich Spread**: Use instead of mayonnaise for an elevated sandwich
- **Pasta Sauce**: Toss with hot pasta for a quick, flavorful meal
- **Meat Enhancer**: Serve alongside grilled chicken, steak, or lamb
- **Toast Topper**: Spread on crusty bread, topped with a drizzle of olive oil
- **Grain Bowl Addition**: Add a dollop to grain bowls for extra flavor

## Traditional vs. Modern

Traditional Catalan romesco uses specific ingredients that might be harder to find outside of Spain:

- **Ñora Peppers**: Small, round, dried red peppers with a sweet flavor
- **Calcots**: A type of spring onion often served with romesco
- **Blanched Almonds**: Used instead of toasted almonds in some traditional recipes

Modern adaptations (like this recipe) substitute more widely available ingredients while maintaining the spirit of the original sauce.

## Variations

### Almond-Only Romesco
Use only almonds instead of the almond-hazelnut mixture for a slightly different flavor profile.

### Extra Smoky Romesco
Add a roasted, peeled poblano or ancho chile to the mixture for additional smokiness and depth.

### Herb-Forward Romesco
Add a larger handful of fresh herbs like parsley, basil, or oregano for a greener, more herbaceous sauce.
