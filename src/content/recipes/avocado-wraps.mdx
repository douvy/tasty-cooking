ingredients:
  - 1 15-oz. can no-salt-added black beans, rinsed and drained
  - 1 fresh jalapeño pepper, seeded and finely chopped
  - 2 tbsp lime juice
  - 1 tsp chili powder
  - ½ tsp ground cumin
  - ¼ tsp garlic powder
  - 4 8- to 9-inch oil-free whole wheat tortillas
  - ½ of a medium red onion, thinly sliced (½ cup)
  - 2 cups shredded fresh spinach
  - ½ cup fresh cilantro leaves
  - 2 mangoes, seeded, peeled, and sliced
  - 1 avocado, halved, seeded, peeled, and sliced
instructions:
  - In a medium bowl, combine the first six ingredients (up to the garlic powder) and coarsely mash with a potato masher or fork.
  - To assemble, spread the black bean mixture over the bottom two-thirds of each tortilla. Top with onion, spinach, mangoes, avocado, and cilantro. Fold in the sides and roll up like a burrito.
notes:
  - This wrap is good for meal prep: make several, wrap them in tin foil, and store in the fridge for an easy lunch.
  - Use oil-free whole wheat tortillas for the healthiest option.
  - Adjust spice level by adding more or less jalapeño pepper.

## A Nutritious Meal in Minutes

Black Bean Avocado Wraps are the perfect solution for busy days when you need a nutritious meal that doesn't sacrifice flavor or satisfaction. Packed with plant-based protein from black beans, healthy fats from avocado, and a rainbow of vegetables, these wraps deliver a complete meal that's as good for your body as it is for your taste buds.

What makes these wraps special is the balance of textures and flavors – creamy avocado, hearty beans, crunchy fresh vegetables, and bright citrus notes from the lime juice. The gentle warming of spices adds depth without overwhelming heat, though you can certainly adjust to your preference with additional cayenne or hot sauce.

These wraps are endlessly customizable based on what you have on hand, making them a perfect clean-out-the-fridge meal that still feels intentional and delicious.

## The Art of Wrapping

Creating the perfect wrap is a skill worth mastering for quick meals that travel well. Here are some tips to ensure your wraps stay intact and delicious:

- **Warm the tortillas**: This makes them more pliable and less likely to crack when folded
- **Create a barrier**: The mashed avocado spread acts as a barrier between the tortilla and wetter ingredients, preventing sogginess
- **Don't overfill**: It's tempting to pile on the filling, but an overstuffed wrap will burst at the seams
- **Tight rolling technique**: Tuck in the sides first, then roll from the bottom up, keeping tension as you go
- **Secure if needed**: If taking to-go, secure with parchment paper, foil, or even a toothpick
- **Toast to seal**: A quick toast in a dry skillet helps seal the wrap and adds a pleasant textural contrast

Remember that the sequence of layering matters – placing sturdy ingredients next to the tortilla helps protect it from tearing when you bite into your wrap.

## Building Flavor Profiles

The basic recipe provides a Mexican-inspired flavor profile, but you can easily adapt these wraps to feature different cuisine styles:

- **Mediterranean**: Use hummus instead of mashed avocado, add cucumber, olives, and feta cheese, and season with za'atar instead of cumin and chili powder
- **Asian-Inspired**: Add thinly sliced cabbage, carrots, and edamame, and season with soy sauce, sesame oil, and a touch of ginger
- **California-Style**: Include sprouts, grated carrots, and cucumber with a tahini-lemon dressing
- **Southwestern**: Add roasted sweet potatoes, pickled jalapeños, and a sprinkle of cheddar cheese

## Variations

### Rainbow Veggie Wraps
Go heavy on the colorful vegetables by adding grated carrots, purple cabbage, yellow squash, and cucumber for a wrap that's as beautiful as it is nutritious.

### Breakfast Wraps
Transform this into a morning meal by adding scrambled eggs or tofu alongside the black bean mixture and topping with salsa.

### Burrito Bowl
Deconstruct the wrap into a bowl format by serving the fillings over a bed of rice or cauliflower rice for a hearty fork-friendly meal.
