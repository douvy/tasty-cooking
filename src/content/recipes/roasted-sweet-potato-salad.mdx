ingredients:
  - 2 lb purple sweet potatoes (6 medium), scrubbed, halved lengthwise
  - 2 tsp avocado oil
  - 1 cup carrot matchsticks (3 in × 1/4 in)
  - ¼ cup thin half-moon slices white or yellow onion
  - 7 ounces yellow pickled radish, cut into 1/4-inch half-moon slices (2 cups)
  - 2 tbsp whole grain mustard
  - 1 tsp kosher salt
  - 1 lemon
instructions:
  - Preheat the oven to 350ºF.
  - Place sweet potatoes cut-sides down on a baking sheet, rub with oil, and roast until tender, 30-40 minutes.
  - While roasting, put carrots and onion in a bowl. Sprinkle with salt, toss, and massage until pliable. Rinse, drain, and return to the bowl. Add pickles, mustard, lemon zest, and juice. Mix well and set aside.
  - Once sweet potatoes are cool enough to handle, scoop the flesh into the bowl (or chop skins if preferred) and fold into the dressing. Season with salt and lemon juice. Serve immediately.
  - Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 1 week.
notes:
  - Unique take on potato salad with no mayo.
  - Uses Korean pickled radish instead of traditional pickle relish.
  - Can be stored in refrigerator for up to 1 week.

## A Perfect Balance of Flavors and Textures

This roasted sweet potato salad combines multiple layers of flavor and texture to create a deeply satisfying dish that works as a main course or a substantial side. The natural sweetness of the roasted sweet potatoes is enhanced by their caramelized edges, while the earthy kale provides a nutritious base that stands up well to the warm potatoes.

The dried cranberries add bursts of tangy sweetness, the pumpkin seeds contribute essential crunch, and the maple-dijon dressing ties everything together with the perfect balance of sweet, tangy, and savory notes.

## The Importance of Massaging Kale

Massaging kale might sound unusual, but it's a crucial step that transforms this hearty green from tough and bitter to tender and flavorful. The process:

1. **Breaks down cell walls**: Making the kale more tender and easier to digest
2. **Reduces bitterness**: Mellowing the flavor considerably
3. **Decreases volume**: Allowing the kale to better absorb the dressing
4. **Enhances color**: Intensifying the deep green hue

Just a minute or two of massaging with a small amount of oil and salt will significantly improve both the texture and flavor of raw kale.

## Serving Suggestions

This versatile salad works beautifully in various settings:

- **Lunch**: Serve on its own for a nutrient-dense meal
- **Dinner**: Pair with grilled fish or roasted chicken
- **Potluck**: Bring to gatherings as a hearty, crowd-pleasing side
- **Meal Prep**: Prepare components separately and assemble for lunches throughout the week
- **Holiday Table**: Offer as a colorful, healthy side dish for Thanksgiving or Christmas

## Seasonal Adaptations

This base recipe can be adjusted to highlight seasonal produce:

### Fall Version
Add 1 cup of roasted Brussels sprouts and substitute maple syrup with apple cider reduction.

### Summer Version
Add 1 cup of fresh corn kernels and substitute dried cranberries with fresh blueberries.

### Winter Version
Add roasted butternut squash alongside the sweet potatoes and incorporate hearty herbs like rosemary and thyme.
