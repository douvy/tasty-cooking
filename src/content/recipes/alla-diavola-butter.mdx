ingredients:
  - ½ pound unsalted butter, at room temperature
  - 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  - 1 tablespoon dried chile flakes
  - 1 tablespoon cracked black pepper
  - ½ teaspoon kosher salt
  - ¼ cup finely chopped seeded pepperoncini (patted dry on paper towels after chopping)
  - 1 tablespoon hot sauce, such as Tabasco
instructions:
  - Fold all the ingredients together with a wooden spoon or rubber spatula and pile into whatever container you want to serve or save it in.
  - Chill the butter for at least 1 hour to firm it up and to let the flavors marry and permeate the butter.
notes:
  - Perfect for melting over steaks, spreading on bread, tossing with pasta, and more.
  - This butter can be frozen for up to 3 months. Wrap well in plastic wrap, then in foil.

## What is Alla Diavola?

"Alla diavola" is an Italian term that translates to "devil's style," indicating the spicy nature of this dish. Traditionally, it refers to a preparation style for meats that incorporates plenty of hot chilies and spices. This compound butter captures those same fiery flavors in a versatile form.

## Serving Suggestions

This spicy compound butter is incredibly versatile in the kitchen:

- **Protein Enhancer**: Place a slice on top of a freshly grilled steak, chicken breast, or fish fillet
- **Bread Upgrade**: Spread on crusty Italian bread for an elevated garlic bread experience
- **Vegetable Finisher**: Melt over grilled or roasted vegetables like corn, asparagus, or zucchini
- **Pasta Addition**: Toss with hot pasta for an instant, spicy sauce
- **Potato Topper**: Melt over baked potatoes or mashed potatoes

## Variations

### Alla Diavola Shrimp Butter
Add 2 tablespoons of finely minced dried shrimp for an umami boost.

### Smoky Alla Diavola Butter
Substitute smoked paprika for regular paprika and add 1/2 teaspoon of liquid smoke.
