Honey Butter Pancakes

Prep: 1 HR 15 min
Ready: 1 HR 45 min
4-6
Breakfast

Fluffy yeasted pancakes served with honey maple butter, finished with lemon zest.

Ingredients

Yeast Mixture (Poolish)

  • 60g warm water
  • 2¼ tsp active dry yeast (1 package/7g)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1¼ cups buttermilk (for flavor and acidity)

Dry Mix

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp sugar (not too sweet)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 5g baking soda (precisely measured)
  • 5g baking powder

Wet Mix

  • 2 large eggs
  • ¼ cup avocado oil (for even browning)

Maple Honey Syrup

  • ½ pound (2 sticks) butter
  • Honey to taste (for Werther's Original-like flavor)
  • Soy sauce to taste (for salty, deep base notes)
  • Maple syrup to taste
  • Salt to taste
  • Touch of water
  • Xanthan gum (optional, for stability)

Whipped Honey Maple Butter

  • ½ pound (2 sticks) room temperature butter
  • Similar flavorings as syrup (honey, maple, etc.)

Berry Compote

  • Equal parts blueberries and strawberries
  • Cut strawberries precisely to blueberry size
  • Sugar (just enough to bring it up)
  • Cornstarch (for thickening)

Garnish

  • Fresh lemon zest

Instructions

  1. Make Poolish (1 hour):
    • Combine warm water (not too hot), buttermilk, and yeast
    • Mix in first cup of flour until just combined
    • Texture should look like cottage cheese - lumps are good
    • Don't overmix or it will be gummy
    • Cover to prevent skin forming
    • Place in warm (not hot) spot for 1 hour
    • When ready, mixture should feel warm to touch and show active bubbles, with slight sour bread aroma
  2. Mix Dry Ingredients:
    • Important: Salt goes in dry mix to avoid killing yeast
    • Combine remaining flour, sugar, salt, precisely measured baking soda and powder
    • Mix until homogeneous
  3. Make Maple Honey Syrup:
    • Gently melt butter (no browning) - microwave okay
    • In separate bowl, combine honey, soy sauce, maple syrup, and salt
    • Mix until completely homogeneous - one solid color
    • Add to melted butter
    • Add touch of water
    • If using xanthan gum, whisk constantly while adding
  4. Make Berry Compote:
    • Cut strawberries precisely to match blueberry size
    • Mix with sugar and cornstarch
    • Let macerate until white/powdery becomes wet/glazey
    • Cook quickly (1-2 minutes) - mixture will be violent and explosive initially
    • Done when berries become limp but maintain shape
    • Immediately shock in ice bath
  5. Make Final Batter:
    • Mix wet ingredients until no yellow streaks
    • Combine with poolish
    • Gently fold in dry ingredients
    • Batter should look "alive" with bubbles forming and popping
    • Should have distinctive sourdough-like aroma
  6. Cook Pancakes:
    • Position rack 6 inches from broiler, preheat broiler
    • Place pan on medium heat for full 5 minutes
    • Test drop should make gentle sizzle, not angry spitting
    • Pour ½ cup batter
    • Cook until bottom is golden brown
    • Move to cooler part for 30 seconds
    • Finish under broiler until top is set and lightly golden
  7. Plate for Visual Impact:
    • Stack two golden-brown pancakes
    • Add vibrant berry compote
    • Add shaped quenelle of honey maple butter
    • Pour syrup generously - pancakes should be "drenched"
    • Finish with fresh lemon zest

Pro Tips: Trust your senses when cooking - sound and smell are as important as sight. The dark golden bottom won't taste burnt. Cut berries exactly blueberry-sized. Add more syrup than seems reasonable. The finished pancake should be a feast for all senses - visual contrasts, varied textures, balanced flavors. Makes 8-10 pancakes (4-5 servings of 2 pancakes each).
